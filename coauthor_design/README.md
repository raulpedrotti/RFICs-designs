[1] A Robust Voltage and Current Stabilization Method for Ultra-Low Voltage Low-Power Inverter-Based RF LNTAs
  - Published in the JICS 2025 edition
  - URL: https://jics.org.br/ojs/index.php/JICS/article/view/951
  - DOI: https://doi.org/10.29292/jics.v20i1.951
  - Abstract: Ultra-low voltage inverter-based low-noise transconductance amplifiers (LNTA) are used for the implementation of energy-efficient short-range RF receivers due to their high equivalent gm/ID ratio. However, they are very sensitive to process, voltage and temperature (PVT) variations, so active biasing circuits are required for proper operation. In this paper, a novel method based on forward bulk biasing is presented to compensate for the variability of the output voltage and drained current of an inverter-based LNTA implementation without the need for a stacked control transistor. To demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method, an LNTA was developed in a 65 nm CMOS process with low-VT deep-nwell transistors operating at a supply voltage of 0.4 V in the 2.4 GHz ISM band. Post layout simulations resulted in a power dissipation of 235 µW, a noise figure of 3.17 dB, a voltage gain of 12.6 dB and a silicon area of 0.11 mm2. The execution of a 1,000-run post-layout Monte Carlo analysis has shown that the proposed biasing method drastically reduces the sensitivity to process variability even considering the supply voltage variation of ± 10% and a temperature range of -40 oC to 100 oC.

[2] An RF-EH Employing Controlled-Impedance Matching for Ultra-Low Voltage Batteryless Devices
  - Attended the 2023 IEEE 14th Latin America Symposium on Circuits and Systems (LASCAS) conference
  - URL: https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/10108099
  - DOI: 10.1109/LASCAS56464.2023.10108099
  - Abstract: Harvesting energy from the existing RF signals present in the environment is a key strategy to power ultra-low voltage (ULV) devices. Such harvesters should present high power conversion efficiency and it is directly affected by the antenna-harvester impedance matching. This paper introduces an RF-EH system with an adapted and digitally-controlled impedance matching approach for batteryless devices. The proposed circuit is designed on a 180-nm CMOS process and is capable of generating a stable supply voltage of 0.4- V from a frequency of 915 MHz while achieving a sensitivity of -19.l6-dBm and 66% power efficiency.
